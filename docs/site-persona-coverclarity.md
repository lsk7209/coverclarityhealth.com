# CoverClarity Site Persona

## Site-topic Persona

Topic scope: Florida ACA Marketplace premium estimates, premium tax credits, subsidy cliffs, cost-sharing reductions, coverage-gap explanations, and methodology for transparent health-insurance cost education.

Excluded topics: plan recommendations, broker lead capture, individualized tax advice, medical advice, government impersonation, scare-based premium content, and ad-first pages.

Primary reader: a Florida resident or family comparing 2026 Marketplace premium costs and trying to understand whether enhanced premium tax credits affect monthly cost.

Reader knowledge level: low to moderate. Explain ACA, PTC, SLCSP, FPL, and CSR on first use. Use plain language and concrete dollar examples.

Search intent patterns: calculator intent, "will my ACA premium go up", "Florida ACA subsidy 2026", "enhanced premium tax credits expired", "subsidy cliff", "Florida Medicaid coverage gap".

Trust standard: YMYL. Every claim about law, eligibility, dates, or federal data needs a source, a date stamp, or a limitation note.

Content promise: show an understandable estimate, explain the assumptions, and point readers to official enrollment channels.

Voice: calm civic editorial. Short sentences. No sales pressure.

Monetization model: AdSense auto ads only. Do not add manual ad slots inside articles, calculators, or CTA areas.

Source policy: prefer IRS, HHS/ASPE, CMS, and HealthCare.gov. Do not cite unsourced policy status claims.

Internal linking rules: each article should link to the calculator, methodology, and at least one related guide.

External linking rules: each policy article should include at least one official external source link.

CTA policy: action-specific and non-salesy. Examples: "Estimate your 2026 premium", "Check the methodology", "Confirm at HealthCare.gov".

Forbidden patterns: quote forms, "get a free quote", fake urgency, government-like seals, excessive keyword repetition, and content created by external writing APIs.

Site-wide quality bar: a page must be useful without ads, readable on mobile, internally linked, dated, and clear about estimate limitations.

## Article-topic Persona

Main keyword: Florida ACA subsidy calculator 2026.

Expanded keywords: enhanced premium tax credits 2026, Florida Marketplace premium estimate, ACA subsidy cliff, cost-sharing reduction, Florida coverage gap.

Search intent: understand whether premiums may rise and estimate the monthly difference.

Reader's immediate problem: "What will I pay in 2026 if the enhanced subsidies are gone?"

Required answer depth: enough to explain the two regimes, FPL thresholds, CSR, coverage gap, and why the calculator is an estimate.

Content format: direct answer, table of contents, concise sections, CTA block, FAQ, official sources.

Internal link targets: home calculator, methodology, about/editorial standards.

External source needs: IRS premium tax credit guidance, HHS poverty guidelines, HealthCare.gov official enrollment.

## Optimization Persona

Priority order: factual accuracy, reader task completion, crawl/indexation, performance, AdSense policy fit, conversion clarity.

Conflict rule: if an SEO phrase makes the content sound like advice or a quote, remove the phrase.

Measurement signals: GSC sitemap success, indexed URLs, query impressions for Florida ACA terms, mobile readability, no layout shift from ads, and no manual ad slots.
